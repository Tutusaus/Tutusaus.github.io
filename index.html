Guillem Tutusaus Alcaraz

Mathematics graduate from Autonomous University of Barcelona

Barcelona, CAT. Mathematics graduate from UAB

Hey there! I’m Guillem Tutusaus Alcaraz, a mathematics graduate from Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona. I grew up in Terrassa, a homely city thirty minutes away from Barcelona.

I would consider myself an individual passionate about Science and Technology. From a young age I always wanted to understand how the planets moved around the sun and why is it that the underlying way to study this is by using calculus. Hence when I had the opportunity to study mathematics I didn’t hesitate on accepting!

Besides my general coursework in Mathematics, throughout the years I’ve taken the initiative to self-study a significant portion of the Computer Science and Physics curriculum. Also, I have experience in teaching most subjects at a Highschool level as well as advanced programming notions.

I enjoy tackling real-world challenges and have had the opportunity to participate in hackathons like UABTheHack and HackUPC, where I worked on problems ranging from optimization in AI algorithms to real-time route planning. My project work is diverse, from developing AI for decision-making using C and heuristic methods to building a low-cost weather balloon capable of recording atmospheric data. Many of these projects are available on my GitHub.

This is my personal website. By clicking on the different tabs above you’ll find a collection of my projects, reports, and anything that sparks my interest in math, engineering, and beyond. For a complete view of my professional experience, you can Download my CV or navigate to the CV tab for a more complete version.

Feel free to explore, and don’t hesitate to reach out if you’d like to connect! Bellow you can find a list of socials networks that I normally use. If you wish to reach me, please send me an email or send me a message on WhatsApp or LinkedIn. I’m always eager to connect with fellow enthusiasts and explore exciting collaborative opportunities.
